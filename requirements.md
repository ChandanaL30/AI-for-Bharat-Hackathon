# Requirements Document – GuidAI

## 1. Problem Statement
In India, a large number of students—especially from tier-2 and tier-3 cities—face difficulty in choosing suitable career paths due to lack of personalized guidance, limited awareness of opportunities, and scattered information across multiple platforms. Existing career guidance systems are mostly generic and do not adapt to individual interests, skills, or educational backgrounds. This often leads to confusion, poor career decisions, and underutilization of student potential.

## 2. Objective
The objective of GuidAI is to provide AI-powered, personalized career and learning guidance to students using natural language interaction. The platform aims to simplify career decision-making and make guidance accessible to underserved student communities.

## 3. Target Users
- School students (classes 10–12)
- Undergraduate students
- Students from tier-2 and tier-3 cities
- Students lacking access to career mentors or counselors

## 4. Functional Requirements
- The system shall allow users to enter career-related queries using natural language.
- The system shall collect user details such as education level, interests, and goals.
- The system shall generate personalized career recommendations using AI.
- The system shall identify skill gaps for recommended career paths.
- The system shall provide curated learning resources (preferably free).
- The system shall display clear next steps for students to follow.

## 5. Non-Functional Requirements
- The system should be easy to use and intuitive.
- The system should respond within a reasonable time.
- The system should be scalable to support a large number of users.
- The system should ensure data privacy and responsible AI usage.
- The system should be accessible on low-end devices and basic internet connections.

## 6. Constraints
- Limited availability of personalized career data for all domains.
- Dependence on external AI/ML APIs.
- Initial scope limited to guidance and recommendations, not guaranteed outcomes.

## 7. Assumptions
- Users provide accurate and honest information.
- Internet connectivity is available.
- Open-source learning resources are accessible.

## 8. Success Metrics
- User satisfaction with career recommendations.
- Reduction in confusion regarding career choices.
- Increased engagement with suggested learning resources.
- Positive feedback from students in underserved regions.
